Agatha Christie

1890 – 1976

About Agatha Christie

Agatha Christie was an English detective novelist and playwright. She wrote some 75 novels, including 66 detective novels and 14 short story collections. Christie is perhaps the world’s most famous mystery writer and is one of the best-selling novelists of all time.

Major Life Events

  1. 1890 – Born in Torquay, England
  2. 1920 – Published her first novel *The Mysterious Affair at Styles*
  3. 1926 – Released *The Murder of Roger Ackroyd*, gaining worldwide fame
  4. 1930 – Introduced Miss Marple in *Murder at the Vicarage*
  5. 1971 – Appointed Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire
  6. 1976 – Died in Oxfordshire, England

Key Contributions

  • Created Hercule Poirot and Miss Marple
  • Wrote 66 detective novels and 14 short story collections
  • Author of *The Mousetrap*, the world’s longest-running play
  • Known as the "Queen of Crime" and best-selling novelist ever
